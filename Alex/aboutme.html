Personal

I am Alex Kalanda, a second year computing student at The African Leadership University in Mauritius

I was born and raised in Kampala, Uganda and am extremely passionate about the use of technology, innovation and entrepreneruship in our daily lives

This has led me to closely work with startups and entrepreneurs from across the continent to find meaningful ways in which they can sustainably grow their businesses while interacting with a diverse range of ecosystems

Internship Project 1

I am working on an initiative with graduate students at the Massachusetts Institute of Technology (MIT) and Harvard to organize the 23rd edition of the MIT Global Startup Workshop(MIT GSW) in a specified location in March 2020 to accelerate technology, innovation and entrepreneurship in the African ecosystem.

The MIT GSW is an initiative from MIT focussing on accelerating entrepreneurship across the world. It is a student led club that has organized this annual edition since 1998 in over 20+ countries on 5 continents and gaining approval from Bill Clinton.

I maintain close communication with other key stakeholders to keep them abreast of the trajectory of our timeline and key developments as well as our next key steps.

Internship Project 2

From 2nd June to 30th August, am currently tasked with building the backend services of the solution. We have so far built up a low-fidelity prototype to test out our offerings to a couple of users.
